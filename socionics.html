Introduction

Socionics is a large body of typology with numerous different schools branching and differing from each other starkly. Socionics was first devised by Aushra Augusta, a Russian economist who was inspired to create a system of sixteen distinct personality types based on Jung's foundational typology work in order to predict the relationships, behaviors, and psychic processes in theoretical spaces. Socionics has garnered wide interest and within the typology community is one of the most popular and widely-used typologies. It has multiple practical applications and ultimately serves as opening one's eyes to the fact that different perspectives and ways of thinking can coexist and function symbiotically.
